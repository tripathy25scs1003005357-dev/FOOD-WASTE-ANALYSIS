📊 Key Findings Summary

The survey reveals that the dominant age group is 18–22 (28%), primarily students (32%) living in 4–5 person households. Most respondents waste food rarely or sometimes, with cooked food being the #1 type wasted (33%) and cooking too much as the top reason (25%). Encouragingly, 38% always try to reduce waste, and 37% always check expiry dates before buying. Only 25 respondents throw away leftovers — most prefer to eat later (24) or give to others (21), showing a positive waste-reduction mindset. Community awareness campaigns, better portion planning, and improved storage practices are the most suggested solutions.
